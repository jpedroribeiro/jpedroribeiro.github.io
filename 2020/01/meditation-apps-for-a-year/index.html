I used meditation apps for a year and here is what I found out

January 05, 2020

I always had some interest in meditation.

In the past, I played around with the idea via yoga classes. It was a mix of positions, breathing exercises and some sort of meditation.

However, my goal at time was purely focused on the physical benefits that yoga would bring to me. Mindfulness was not even in the picture.

In fact, mindfulness was not even I word I was aware of.

Fast-forward to a few years, the trend of meditation or mindfulness apps has peaked (over 100 apps last time I checked), and meditation on the go has never been so easy.

So, I decided to give it a go to see what being mindful means, and how would it change me (if at all).

The Apps

After some consideration, I decided to go with Aura & Headspace. Mainly because I had an extended trial for both that would allow me to fully use their app without the free tier limitations.

The Good, the bad & the ugly

Gamification

Aura uses day streak, which makes no sense to me. It only serves to pressure the user in using the app for the sake of it and induces anxiety rather than mitigates it.

Headspace takes it easy and only tracks total minutes meditating. It feels positive regardless of what that number means to you.

Day to day use

Aura has loads of instructors. Each tackling different topics ranging from gratitude to life stories.

Headspace feels more like a continuous learning experience. The tracks (mostly) have an end goal. Like chapters on a book.

I found that Aura was best for one-offs - that moment when you just need to sit down and get it going.

Taking the time to gather your thoughts and be mindful, even for a few minutes a day, does work. At least it did for me.

I‚Äôve noticed the biggest change in the first 2-3 weeks.

If you have never done mindfulness before and just want to have a taste of it, go for Aura. Have a couple sessions and see how you feel.
